Hyde Park is the home of the University of Chicago, so it's better for books than beer. The Seminary Coop is one of the best bookstores in the world, and Powell's has an excellent selection of second-hand books. There are also five museums close by: the Museum of Science and Industry, the Oriental Institute, the Robie House, the Smart Museum and the Renaissance Society. The best sandwiches are at Z&H; fancy French food can be found at La Petite Folie but there are various other options on 53rd, 55th and 57th Streets. For a beer try Jimmy's Woodlawn Tap or The Cove. Rent bikes at the 53rd St. Bike Center or, best of all, just stroll and swim at Promontory Point. See the map for precise locations.

There's a ton to do in Chicago, obviously, but here are some suggestions:

  • The Art Institute
  • Millennium Park
  • The Riverboat Architecture Tour
  • The Green Mill (jazz)
  • The Hancock Center (go for a drink at the top)
  • Lincoln Park zoo (free!)
  • Lao Szechuan (Chinese Food)
  • Avec (Small Plates)
  • The Hopleaf (bar with huge selection)
